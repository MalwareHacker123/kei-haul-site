Do you assemble or disassemble furniture and appliances?

We’re set up to move your items safely, not to rebuild complex systems in the field. That means we generally won’t take apart stoves, intricate bed frames, antique pieces with fragile joinery, or stacked washer/dryer setups that need specialized disassembly to avoid damage or warranty issues.

If you’re unsure whether your item falls in that bucket, the fastest path is a photo (front + hookups if it’s an appliance) and a short note about what you need disconnected. We’ll tell you plainly if it’s something we can’t touch, or if simple steps—like doors off hinges or a quick unhook you’ve already had done by a pro—are enough for us to haul it.

That policy exists so we don’t create liability for your home, your equipment, or our crew mid-job. When in doubt, ask before move day.

How does mileage pricing work?

Your total estimate is built from several pieces: item pricing (by size category), stairs/floors, weight add-ons when a single item is very heavy, optional difficulty factors (long carries, tight turns), and mileage between pickup and drop-off.

For mileage specifically: the first 10 miles are free. After that, each additional mile is $0.50. We use the addresses or zip codes you give us to estimate that segment up front so rural or outer-metro jobs don’t feel like a black box.

If your route changes meaningfully (extra stop, different town), tell us before we dispatch so we can update the number. The goal is predictable math, not surprise add-ons at the door.

What should I send for a fast, accurate estimate?

Think in terms of where, what, and how hard the path is. The “where” is pickup and drop-off—full addresses are best, but at minimum both zip codes plus neighborhood context (e.g. “Cap Hill walk-up” vs “suburban driveway”).

The “what” is your item list with rough sizes: couch length, fridge type, whether something is one piece or multiple pieces, and approximate weight if you know it (especially over ~200 lb for a single item, since that triggers add-on bands on our sheet).

The path matters: floor count at each end, elevator or not, parking situation, and any tight hallways or stair turns. Photos taken from the approach (hall + doorway) beat long text every time. With that bundle, we can usually reply with a clear range or a firm quote before we book the window.

  • Photos: item + stairs + any doorway that looks “snug”
  • Timing: preferred day part (morning vs afternoon) if you care
  • Contact: call, text, or email—whatever’s fastest for you
